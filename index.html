COMPLETED PROJECTS

Self-Balancing Motorcycle

The design and fabrication of a rapid prototype for a self-balancing motorcycle involve multiple stages, including the determination of design requirements, mechanical and electronic system design, prototype fabrication, and testing to ensure performance standards are met. The mechanical design consists of the frame, suspension, wheels, and steering system, while the electronic design integrates control systems, sensors, and motors. Fabrication techniques such as 3D printing, CNC machining, and welding are employed to manufacture the components. These components are assembled to form the prototype, which can then be used for further development and optimization toward a fully functional self-balancing motorcycle
Self-balancing mechanism Gyroscope Sensor

Air Engine

Environmental pollution in metropolitan cities is rising rapidly, primarily due to the increased use of fossil fuel-powered vehicles. One promising alternative is a compressed air-powered engine. The key advantage of this engine is that it does not require hydrocarbon fuel, thus eliminating combustion and emissions. In this project, a four-stroke single-cylinder spark ignition (SI) engine is converted into a two-stroke engine that operates using compressed air. This modification makes it simple, cost-effective, and easy to adapt since it utilizes existing engine structures. Air, being abundant and renewable, serves as a clean and sustainable fuel source for this innovative system.
Compressed air Non-combustion engine Renewable energy

in Automobiles

The cruise control system is a crucial component in modern automotive control engineering, enabling a vehicle to maintain a constant speed without continuous driver input. This project focuses on designing a mechanical cruise control lever that can sustain a constant vehicle speed, especially on long journeys and inclined roads. The proposed system improves driving comfort and reduces fatigue. Cruise control continues to be an essential feature in vehicles for ensuring consistent performance and driver convenience.

Parallel Parking System

This project proposes a manual driving system for a four-wheel independent steering and driving vehicle to facilitate efficient parallel parking. The system aims to reduce human error and collisions that often occur during parking in limited spaces. A small-scale prototype is developed to demonstrate the design’s feasibility and functionality. The results indicate that the developed model can identify suitable parking spaces, execute parallel parking maneuvers, avoid front and rear obstacles, and significantly reduce parking time.
Driving system Parking Parallel parking Autonomous maneuver

Pneumatic Automatic Hand

Brake Release System

The handbrake is a vital safety component in vehicles. Traditional handbrake systems rely on manual engagement using a ratchet locking mechanism, which may lead to accidents if not properly activated. To address this issue, a pneumatic automatic handbrake system is proposed. The system employs an electric motor and pneumatic actuator to engage and release the handbrake automatically, minimizing driver error. This design is simple, efficient, and cost-effective, enhancing both safety and convenience.
Handbrake Pneumatics Safety system Pressure regulator

Pneumatic Jack

The term pneumatic originates from the Greek word pneuma, meaning "air" or "breath." Pneumatics refers to the use of compressed air to perform mechanical work. This project involves the design and fabrication of a pneumatic jack, which uses pressurized air to lift heavy loads efficiently. Compared to conventional jacks, the pneumatic jack is easier to operate, faster, and safer. It demonstrates the importance of pneumatics in modern automation and mechanical handling systems.
Pneumatic Mechanical automation Jack Compressed air

Pneumatic Vice

A vice is a mechanical device used to hold workpieces securely during machining operations such as drilling, sawing, or milling. This project focuses on developing an automatic pneumatic vice that provides a quick and efficient clamping mechanism using compressed air. The system ensures accurate alignment, consistent pressure, and reduced manual effort. The pneumatic vice enhances productivity, precision, and safety in machining operations.
Pneumatic vice Automation Clamping Machining

Floating Garbage

Cleaning System

Plastic waste pollution poses a severe threat to aquatic environments and human health. This project presents the design and fabrication of a manually controlled floating garbage cleaning system capable of collecting plastic and floating waste from water surfaces. The system is mobile, flexible, and energy-efficient, making it suitable for cleaning lakes, rivers, and other water bodies. Experimental results demonstrate the prototype’s effectiveness in collecting and disposing of floating waste, contributing to a cleaner and more sustainable environment.

Timed Speed Breaker

Power Generation System

This project focuses on designing and fabricating a timed speed breaker capable of generating electricity through vehicular motion. When a vehicle passes over the speed breaker, it compresses the mechanism, which in turn rotates a flywheel connected to a generator to produce electricity. The generated power can be used immediately or stored for later use. The system design utilizes 3D modeling software for structural and mechanical analysis. Materials such as metal frames, sheet metal, shafts, and wiper motors are employed for fabrication. This innovative setup demonstrates a sustainable and cost-effective approach to energy generation from vehicular movement.
Timed speed breaker Power generation Sustainable energy Innovative design
